Founding Story

Our Vision from Day One

Haristar Envirotech was founded with a singular vision: to eliminate the risk that hazardous chemical wastewater poses to Gujarat's soil, groundwater, and communities. Established in 2026 at Makarpura GIDC, Vadodara — one of India's most active industrial clusters — we set out to provide shared, accessible hazardous waste treatment solutions.

Our Common MEE (Multiple Effect Evaporation) facility bridges the gap for small and medium enterprises who need compliant waste treatment but cannot justify standalone infrastructure costs. We provide that infrastructure at competitive prices.

India generates approximately 7.8 million tonnes of hazardous industrial waste annually. Regulations are tightening and enforcement is strengthening. Haristar exists to make responsible, compliant waste treatment accessible across Gujarat's industrial ecosystem.

Core Service

Common MEE Facility

⚗️

Common MEE Facility — Hazardous Wastewater Evaporation

Our flagship offering is a Common Multiple Effect Evaporation (MEE) Plant — a shared industrial facility allowing multiple companies to process their hazardous liquid waste through a single, high-capacity, and fully compliant treatment system. The MEE process significantly reduces the volume of industrial effluents through controlled evaporation, converting hazardous wastewater into concentrated solids for safe disposal. This eliminates the need for each company to build expensive standalone treatment infrastructure.

Licenses & Authorizations

Our Compliance Infrastructure

🏛️Pollution Control Board Authorization

Haristar Envirotech operates under full authorization from the Gujarat Pollution Control Board (GPCB) / SPCB — permitting legal receipt, storage, treatment, and disposal of hazardous industrial waste from registered client industries across the region.

🌿Environmental Clearance

All facilities and operations at Haristar are designed to comply with Environmental Clearance (EC) requirements under the Environment (Protection) Act — ensuring our plant meets national environmental protection standards.

⚠️Hazardous Waste Handling License

We hold the requisite Hazardous Waste Handling License under the Hazardous Waste Management, Handling & Transboundary Movement Rules — covering collection, storage, treatment, and scientifically compliant disposal of hazardous waste.

🏭Factory & Industrial Safety Certification

Our facility maintains all factory and industrial safety certifications — including compliance with occupational health & safety requirements for handling hazardous chemicals and effluents under the Factories Act.

📋Annual Returns to SPCB/PCC

As mandated by regulation, Haristar submits comprehensive annual returns to the State Pollution Control Board confirming all treatment methods, quantities of waste handled, and disposal records — maintaining full transparency and regulatory traceability.

Rapid Sector Growth

India generates approximately 62 million tonnes of waste annually, with hazardous and industrial waste growing rapidly. Recycling and waste technology markets are projected to reach multi-billion dollar levels by 2030 — creating strong long-term demand for scientific treatment facilities.
